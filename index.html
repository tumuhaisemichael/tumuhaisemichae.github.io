Tailor-made Expedition Safaris (TES)

Explore the flexibility and customization of our custom safaris

Custom Safaris

Tailor-made or customised safaris are safaris that are put together by the customer, or a travel specialist, or by both, and are privately managed. We correspond with our potential guests to organise their expedition safaris based on their needs and finances. This provides flexibility in many ways as dates of travel can be decided by the guests, also the type of accommodation and budget. It is also possible to mix camping and lodge accommodation. The choice of safari destination is not limited to popular areas. It is possible to mix and match destinations to create a unique and often more rewarding safari experience. Making your own safari gives you the opportunity to travel at your own pace and to enjoy the interests that you have.

  • You have the ability to have a say in the lodges and camps that you will be staying.
  • The client can choose the accommodation from the options provided that they are going to be staying at during the trip.
  • Client accommodation taste differs; some people prefer a more rustic and authentic African experience while others prefer a more comfortable and luxurious room.
  • Clients have control over the safari's budget. In a tailor-made safari, the client has the power to control the budget because the trip is built around their specific needs. For example, the client may state that they want to see the wildebeest migration in Tanzania, however, they do not want to spend a lot of money. In this situation, the travel consultant would find an affordable way for the client to experience the migration, such as looking at the different options for accommodation and using a more economic, but still reliable, safari vehicle. This can only be achieved in a custom-made safari.

Medical Profession Expeditions (MPE)

Explore medical expeditions in Uganda and East Africa

Intended Audience

Intended for paramedics, nurses, medical physicians, students, and other healthcare professionals seeking to expand their knowledge and enhance their medical expertise.

Specialization

We specialize in coordinating top-tier medical camps, hospital visits, laboratory visits, medical field safaris, and field projects.

Medical Camps

Rural areas in Africa lack access to regular medical care. Medical camps established by international NGOs, volunteers, and well-wishers frequently serve as a temporary solution, allowing those in isolated areas to access medical care after a prolonged period without seeing a doctor. We coordinate the dispatch of medical personnel from both local and international teams, equipped with medicine and specialized knowledge.

Visits to Hospital and Public Health Laboratories

We organize visits to both public and private hospitals and laboratories. Our knowledgeable medical team guides you through our nationwide public health and laboratory systems. This can be for both experiential and educational services.

Medical Supplies

We request well-wishers, volunteers, corporate organizations, NGOs, enterprises, and other entities to contribute medical supplies to support our local rural health clinics and laboratories, which are now facing a critical shortage.

African Traditional Medicine Expeditions

Uganda and most of East Africa boast of active traditional medical healing through the use of local herbs and ‘African chemistry’. Interact with known African medicine men to appreciate what it means to use African traditional knowledge, African-originated ideas, and historical, philosophical, and cultural means of tackling health issues.

Advancing One Health

Advancing One Health necessitates collaborative efforts that address both animal and human health. The One Health concept is a global public health strategy. We coordinate and integrate approaches for sharing what each health profession has to offer, which will advance One Health across the globe.

Key Areas of Advancing One Health

  • Medical internships and student exchange programs in Africa
  • Study of disease dynamics at the animal-human-environment interface
  • Tropical medicine and One health research
  • Neglected Tropical Diseases
  • Communicable diseases
  • Non-communicable diseases

Other Areas to Consider

  • The impact of salt mining in Lake Katwe on both female and male fertility
  • Salt mining at Lake Katwe has been linked to cancer
  • Vitiligo and other stigmatized conditions
  • Synaesthesia and other traits

Study Abroad and Field Rotations Programs

Explore study abroad opportunities in Uganda and East Africa

Program Details

Detailed program available on request. We invite students and recent graduates to take part in our study abroad programmes showcasing health and cultural diversity in Uganda and East Africa. We are open to individuals and groups seeking internships and externships, clinical rotations, and work away activities (volunteering).

Signature Program: COVAB-Makerere Annual Study Abroad Programme

This programme exposes students to aspects including livestock health, production, and management; wildlife health and management; veterinary public health; veterinary and human diagnostic laboratories; food science and nutrition; human public health systems; environmental conservation; and culture. It aims to build a team that will advance a 'One Health' approach to promote health at the Human-Animal-Environment interface.

Veterinary Ecological Safaris

Visit Uganda and East Africa and work with our experienced wildlife and domestic animal veterinarians to study both the practical and theoretical elements of:

  • Wildlife veterinary medicine and conservation in Africa
  • Wildlife search, rescue, and rehabilitation, e.g., snare removals and treatment
  • Interaction of wild and domestic animals and the implications of that
  • Human-wildlife conflicts
  • Park management
  • Disease monitoring and surveillance
  • Field laboratory settings
  • Free-living aquatic animal health
  • Herptile management and therapeutics

Wildlife Immobilisation Course ($2,850) in Uganda

Our signature course is a 14-day training course (Field Techniques for Zoo and Wildlife Capture) designed for veterinary students and veterinarians, but other professionals are invited to participate. The course can also be tailored to the individual needs of a group, and should thus be regarded as a guideline for what is feasible.

Experiential Tourism in Uganda and East Africa

You can also participate in our experiential tourism with local veterinarians and researchers to track large predators (e.g., lions and leopards) and other wildlife using satellite technology. This allows you to detour inside the national park to have an up-close encounter with wildlife and take the best videos and photos.

Field Projects in Uganda and East Africa

We welcome individual and group projects addressing issues at the interface between animals, humans, and the environment. SFES will help in the processing of research permits, national park entry permits, introduction letters to community leaders, materials transfer agreements (MTAs), and other necessary documents relating to the efficient running of your projects in Uganda and East Africa.
